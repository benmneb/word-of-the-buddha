\[SN 12.43] But with the remainderless fading away and cessation of that same wanting (tanhā) comes cessation of fuel (upādāna); with the cessation of fuel, cessation of states of existence (bhava); with the cessation of states of existence, cessation of rebirth (jāti); with the cessation of rebirth, ageing-and-death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, unhappiness, and distress cease. Such is the cessation of this whole mass of suffering. This is the passing away of suffering.

\[SN 22.30] The cessation, subsiding, and passing away of form …of experience (vedanā)… of perception … of will … of consciousnesses …is the cessation of suffering, the subsiding of affliction, the passing away of ageing-and-death.
