The one who sees the Dhamma sees Dependant Origination. The one who sees Dependant Origination sees the Dhamma. [MN 28.38]

[SN 12.1] With delusion as cause, will[comes to be];<br />
with will as cause, consciousnesses;<br />
with consciousness as the cause, name-and-form (the objects of consciousnesses);<br />
with name-and-form as the cause, the six sense bases;<br />
with the six sense bases as cause, sensory contact;<br />
with sensory contact as cause, experience;<br />
with experience as cause, wanting;<br />
with wanting as cause, fuel;<br />
with fuel as cause, states of existence;<br />
with states of existence as cause, rebirth;<br />
with rebirth as cause, ageing-and-death, sorrow, crying, pain, unhappiness, and distress come to be.

Such is the origin of this whole mass of suffering.

[SN 12.51] But when a meditator has abandoned delusion and aroused true knowledge, then, with the fading away of delusion and the arising of true knowledge, one does not generate a meritorious volition, or a demeritorious volition, or a neutral volition.

[SN 12.1] But with the remainderless fading away and cessation of delusion comes cessation of will;<br />
with the cessation of will, cessation of consciousnesses;<br />
with the cessation of consciousnesses, cessation of name-and-form;<br />
with the cessation of name-and-form, cessation of the six sense bases;<br />
with the cessation of the six sense bases, cessation of sensory contact;<br />
with the cessation of sensory contact, cessation of experience;<br />
with the cessation of experience, cessation of wanting;<br />
with the cessation of wanting, cessation of fuel;<br />
with the cessation of fuel, cessation of states of existence;<br />
with the cessation of states of existence, cessation of rebirth;<br />
with the cessation of rebirth, ageing-and-death, sorrow, crying, pain, unhappiness, and distress cease.

Such is the cessation of this whole mass of suffering.
